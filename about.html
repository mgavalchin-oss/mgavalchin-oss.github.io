About Me

Designer

Hi, I’m Max Gavalchin — a graphic designer from New York City currently pursuing his studies at the Rhode Island School of Design. Working across motion graphics, print publications, and web design, Max is deeply interested in how visual systems can be constructed and sometimes intentionally disrupted to communicate a specific message. He thinks of design as a language shaped by relationships: between type and image, rhythm and pacing, structure and spontaneity. In both digital and physical formats, Max builds frameworks that guide understanding. Whether developing a grid for a publication, choreographing motion for a sequence, or mapping interactions for a website, he uses system-based thinking to determine how viewers move through content. His knowledge of coding languages such as JavaScript strengthens this approach, enabling him to craft responsive and interactive experiences where design logic and user behavior intersect. Max continues to explore how thoughtful systems can make complex ideas legible, engaging, and memorable.

My work focuses on responsive layouts, modern UI patterns, and writing maintainable, scalable code. Whether it’s a portfolio site or a full web application, I care deeply about detail and user experience.

Outside of coding, I enjoy exploring new design trends, refining animations, and continuously learning new tools to improve my workflow.
